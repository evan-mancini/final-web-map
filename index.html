Debunking Transit Deserts

A transit desert is an area with inadequate public transportation options. In New York, many think of neighborhoods without subway stations as transit deserts. However, this notion assumes that the subway is the only transit mode and ignores the bus and rail systems that serve the city. This map examines Bayside, Marine Park, and Maspeth, three neighborhoods that lack immediate subway access and are often called transit deserts. Click the buttons below to explore them.

The map initial shows only the subway routes, but by clicking the buttons above, the subway routes are replaced by the bus and rail networks, revealing the forgotten transit network. The map also shows how far you can travel in 45 minutes from the center of each neighborhood using only buses and commuter rail. Are these really transit deserts?
